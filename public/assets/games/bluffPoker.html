Bluff Poker(Creator unknown). Rules updated by Bharat Ponnaluri

Overview of game:

Bluff poker is a combination of bluff and poker where players will bet on increasingly strong hands. Every time a player is caught bluffing or incorrectly says that someone is bluffing, they get a point. A player is eliminated from the game when they have too many points.

Setting up the game for the first round

  • Agree on the maximum number of points a player can have before they are eliminated.
  • Shuffle a standard 52 card deck and deal 1 card to each player.
  • Pick a random start player.

Playing a round:

  • The start player picks a poker hand, and names one card in that hand which has the highest value. If a player is claiming a flush is present, they must also name the suit of the highest value card. When naming a hand, a player is claiming that the hand can be made from the cards in everyone's hand.
  • Play continues clockwise. Players can either name a higher value hand or call bluff on the previous claim.
  • If a player calls a bluff, then everyone reveals their hand. If the claimed hand can be made from the revealed cards, then the player who called the bluff gets a point. If not, then the player who made the incorrect claim gets two points, and the player who called the bluff loses a point.

What to do after a round:

  • If a player has more points than the maximum, they are eliminated from the game. If only one player remains, they win.
  • Shuffle all 52 cards, Deal one card to each player for each point they have. If a player has 0 points or negative points, they do not get any cards.
  • Set the start player to the one who most recently got a point.

Advice for playing:

  • Consider the number of cards people have when claiming a hand. For example, on the first round of a 4 player game, everyone will only have 1 card, which makes it impossible for a straight to be present.
  • Estimate the probabilities of certain hands being present. On the first round of a 5 player game, the chances of a pair being present is approximately 50%. The odds of correctly picking a number of the card in the pair is lower.
  • Later in the game, the probability of higher value hands increases significantly. As a result, opening with a higher value claim such as "Flush, high card ace of spades." becomes a good idea.
